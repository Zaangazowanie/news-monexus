# Lead
Iranian drones struck the headquarters of an Iranian Kurdish opposition group in northern Iraq on Wednesday, wounding two fighters and opening a new front in a war that's rapidly expanding beyond Iran's borders.

# What Happened
- Target: Iranian Kurdish opposition HQ, Dekala (Iraq Kurdistan)
- Weapon: Suicide drone
- Strike: Arms depot hit
- Casualties: 2 fighters wounded
- Date: Wednesday, March 4

# The Pattern
- Feb 28: Iran strikes Kurdistan Region (ballistic missiles)
- Mar 1: More strikes on PDKI bases
- Mar 4: Drone strike on Dekala HQ

# What's Being Hidden
- Kurdish groups say they're conducting operations inside Iran
- Iran using war as cover to settle scores with internal opposition
- Iraq's government unable to control its own territory
- US forces concentrated in Kurdistan region

# The Kicker
The war started with Israel and the US. Now Iran is fighting on four fronts — including against its own Kurdish minority in exile.
