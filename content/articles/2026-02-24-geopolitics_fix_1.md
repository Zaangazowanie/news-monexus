## The Strategic Waterway Under Siege

The Red Sea, conduit for approximately 12% of global trade and 30% of container shipping, has become the focal point of escalating maritime conflict. Houthi rebel attacks on commercial vessels have forced unprecedented shipping route diversions.

## Attack Patterns and Escalation

The Iran-backed Houthi movement has conducted over 150 attacks on merchant vessels using anti-ship missiles, drones, and explosive-laden boats. These attacks represent a novel form of asymmetric warfare.

## Economic Consequences

### Shipping Cost Surge

Container shipping rates from Asia to Europe have increased 340% since October 2024. The diversion of vessels around Africa's Cape of Good Hope adds approximately 10 days and $1 million in fuel costs per journey.

### Energy Market Impacts

QatarEnergy has suspended Red Sea transits, redirecting cargoes around Africa and tightening European gas supply.

## International Response

### Operation Prosperity Guardian

The United States initiated a multinational naval coalition in December 2024. U.S. Central Command has conducted over 400 strikes against Houthi targets.

### European Union Naval Force

The European Union launched Operation Aspides in February 2025, deploying warships from France, Germany, Italy, and Greece.

### Chinese Position

China has pursued diplomatic engagement with Iran while increasing PLA Navy presence in the Gulf of Aden.

## Regional Power Dynamics

### Iranian Strategy

The Houthi campaign serves Iranian strategic objectives by demonstrating Tehran's capacity to disrupt global commerce.

### Saudi Arabia's Dilemma

Saudi Arabia has pursued cautious engagement, providing intelligence support while avoiding direct military strikes.

## Long-Term Strategic Implications

### Maritime Security Architecture

The Red Sea crisis exposes vulnerabilities in international maritime security frameworks designed for state-based threats.

### Supply Chain Restructuring

Extended disruption may accelerate supply chain diversification away from Suez-dependent routing.

## Conclusion

The Red Sea crisis represents a significant stress test for international maritime security frameworks and global trade resilience.
