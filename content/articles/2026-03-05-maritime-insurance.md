# Lead
The world's largest maritime insurance mutuals are cancelling war risk cover for ships entering the Persian Gulf effective March 5 — a move that will force tankers to either run uninsured or stay out of the Strait of Hormuz entirely, potentially spiking global energy costs.

# The Stakes
- Insurers: Gard, Skuld, NorthStandard, London P&I Club, American Club
- Vessels affected: 150+ anchored outside Hormuz
- Insurance rates: 50-100% jump
- Oil transit: ~20% of global supply through Hormuz

# The Cascade Effect
1. Insurance withdrawn → Ships can't enter Gulf without cover
2. Tankers stay out → Oil/LNG supply constrained
3. Freight rates double → Shipping costs spike
4. Energy costs rise → Consumer prices increase

# What's Being Hidden
- "Force majeure" clauses protect insurers, not consumers
- Cost transfer: Insurance → shipping → checkout prices
- Just-in-time supply chains can't absorb Gulf shutdown

# The Kicker
The war is fought with missiles. The supply chain damage is done with insurance cancellations. Consumers pay for both.
