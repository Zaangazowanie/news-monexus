# Lead

Elon Musk's X Money payment app has entered limited beta, with William Shatner — the original Captain Kirk — as its highest-profile tester. The screenshots reveal cashback, 6% yield on deposits, and FDIC insurance. What they don't reveal is why anyone should trust Musk with their money.

# The Pitch

- 6% APY on deposits (vs ~4% high-yield savings)
- Cashback on card purchases
- FDIC insured up to $250K via Cross River Bank
- Beta invites auctioned for charity ($1,000 each)

# The "Everything App" Vision

Musk wants X to be "the central source of all monetary transactions" — payments, messaging, AI (Grok), identity, content. All your digital life, one platform.

# What's Being Hidden

- Where does the 6% yield come from? (Banks don't pay that without risk)
- What data does X Money collect and how does it connect to X's ad business?
- What happens when your "everything app" account gets suspended?
- Crypto integration remains "a mystery"
- **Data harvesting:** What happens to transaction data? (X's track record isn't great)
- **Lock-in:** Once your money is in the "everything app," how easy is it to leave?
- **Risk concentration:** Banking + social media + AI + identity = single point of failure

# Incentive Mapping

- **Musk/X:** Wants transaction fees, user lock-in, financial data
- **Beta testers:** Getting exclusive access, yield arbitrage, status
- **Regulators:** Watching from the sidelines (for now)
- **Users:** 6% yield (if it lasts), convenience, X integration

# Critical Questions

1. Is X Money a bank? A payment processor? A crypto wallet? All three?
2. What jurisdiction governs disputes?
3. When X inevitably has a content moderation dispute, what happens to your money?
4. Who owns Cross River Bank and what's their relationship to Musk?
5. What happens to your money if X goes bankrupt?

# Kicker

The yield is the hook. The lock-in is the product. The risk is everything they're not telling you. One man wants to be your bank, your social network, your AI assistant, and your identity provider. What could go wrong?
