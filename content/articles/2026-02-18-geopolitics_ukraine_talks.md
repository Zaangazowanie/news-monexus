## Historic Meeting in Riyadh

President Donald Trump and Russian President Vladimir Putin met for three hours in Riyadh, Saudi Arabia on February 15, 2026, in the highest-level U.S.-Russian engagement since the 2022 Ukraine invasion. The summit, hosted by Crown Prince Mohammed bin Salman, produced agreement to establish negotiating teams to explore a "framework for peace," though no ceasefire was announced.

Trump described the meeting as "productive and very frank" in post-summit remarks, while Putin characterized discussions as "constructive without being naive." Both leaders agreed to direct their respective Ukraine teams to meet within ten days in Geneva, with follow-up presidential contact planned depending on progress.

## European Anxiety

The unilateral U.S.-Russian engagement without Ukrainian or European participation has triggered alarm across NATO capitals. Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy criticized the summit as "appeasement," warning that any agreement reached without Ukrainian input would lack legitimacy and durability.

European Union leaders convened an emergency summit in Brussels on February 17, expressing concern that Trump might accept territorial concessions to Russia in exchange for economic concessions or security guarantees unrelated to Ukraine. French President Emmanuel Macron emphasized that "Europe must be at the table when decisions affecting European security are made."

## Negotiation Framework

Leaked documents suggest the negotiating teams will consider several contentious issues:

- Recognition of Russian control over Crimea and portions of eastern Ukraine
- Ukrainian neutrality and exclusion from NATO membership
- Lifting of Western sanctions in phases tied to Russian compliance
- Security guarantees for Ukraine's remaining territory
- Status of Russian-speaking populations in contested regions

Ukrainian officials have rejected any territorial concessions as non-negotiable, while Russia maintains its maximalist demands for Ukrainian "denazification" and demilitarization.

## Military Reality

The battlefield situation heavily favors Russia as talks commence. Ukrainian forces have lost significant territory in Donbas over the past six months, while Western military aid has declined following the 2024 U.S. election and European capacity constraints. Ukraine's mobilization difficulties have left frontline units understrength and exhausted.

Russian military production has expanded dramatically, with artillery ammunition output now exceeding Western supply to Ukraine by a 7:1 margin. Iranian and North Korean weapons deliveries have supplemented domestic production, while Western sanctions have failed to significantly constrain Russian defense manufacturing.

## Economic Considerations

Trump has linked Ukraine negotiations to broader economic arrangements, suggesting that normalized U.S.-Russian commercial relations could follow a peace agreement. American energy companies have reportedly prepared proposals for Russian oil and gas partnerships should sanctions be lifted.

European officials worry that economic incentives may drive Trump toward premature settlement. German Chancellor Friedrich Merz warned that "sacrificing Ukraine for commercial opportunities would destroy the rules-based international order and embolden aggressors everywhere."

## Ukrainian Dilemma

Zelenskyy faces unpalatable choices. Continued war risks catastrophic military defeat and further territorial losses. Negotiated settlement on unfavorable terms would represent political suicide and potential national humiliation. European security guarantees, however sincerely offered, cannot replace the military assistance Ukraine has received from the United States.

The Ukrainian president has indicated willingness to consider neutrality and postpone NATO aspirations in exchange for robust security guarantees and continued economic support. However, acceptance of territorial losses would violate constitutional provisions and likely trigger domestic political crisis.

## What Comes Next

The Geneva negotiations will test whether any mutually acceptable settlement exists. Russia's military advantage suggests minimal incentive for compromise, while Ukraine's existential commitment to sovereignty limits concession possibilities. Trump's transactional approach may prioritize demonstrable "deal" over sustainable peace.

As talks commence, fighting continues across eastern Ukraine with mounting casualties on both sides. The gap between diplomatic process and battlefield reality underscores the immense challenge of ending Europe's bloodiest conflict since World War II—a challenge that will test the international community's capacity for coherent crisis management and moral clarity.
