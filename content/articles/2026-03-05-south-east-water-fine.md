# Lead
South East Water is facing a £22 million fine from Ofwat for supply failures during high demand periods last year, with the regulator saying the company left customers facing "immense stress."

# What Happened
- Company: South East Water
- Fine: £22 million
- Issue: Supply failures during high demand
- Regulator: Ofwat

# Ofwat's Statement
"The firm was unable to cope during high demand, leading to immense stress for customers who were left without water at critical times."

# What's Being Hidden
- Infrastructure investment has been deferred for years
- Climate change is making extremes more common
- Executive bonuses weren't linked to resilience

# The Kicker
A £22m fine is pocket change for a water company. The real cost is borne by families left thirsty.
