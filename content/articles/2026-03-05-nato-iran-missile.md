# Lead
A ballistic missile launched from Iran was shot down by NATO air defense assets over Turkish airspace on Wednesday — marking the first direct engagement of NATO forces in the escalating Iran-Israel war.

# What Happened
- Timing: Wednesday, March 4
- Significance: First NATO involvement in conflict

# Turkish Defense Ministry Statement
"A ballistic munition detected to have been launched from Iran and, after passing through the airspace of Iraq and Syria, directed towards Turkish airspace, was timely engaged and neutralized by NATO air and missile defense assets deployed in the Eastern Mediterranean."

# The Expansion
- Turkey said it will NOT allow airspace for attacks on Iran
- Incirlik air base hosts US forces but Turkey maintaining neutrality
- Missile crossed Iraq, Syria before reaching Turkish airspace
- NATO forced to defend member state territory

# Key Questions
1. Was Turkey the target or collateral damage?
2. Does this trigger Article 5 consultation?
3. What happens if Iran launches more missiles toward Turkey?

# The Kicker
NATO just shot down its first Iranian missile. The alliance is now in the war — whether it wanted to be or not.
