## The Crisis Deepens

Manchester United's turbulent 2025-26 season reached a critical juncture in February 2026 as manager Ruben Amorim faced mounting pressure following a dismal run of results that has left the club languishing in the bottom half of the Premier League table. The Portuguese coach, who arrived with significant fanfare to replace the sacked Erik ten Hag in November 2025, has struggled to implement his preferred 3-4-3 system with a squad ill-suited to his tactical demands.

## The Numbers Tell a Story

United's statistics under Amorim make grim reading for supporters. In his 15 Premier League matches at the helm, the club has secured just four victories, drawing five and losing six. The team's goal difference stands at a miserable minus eight, with defensive frailties exposing the limitations of a back three that lacks both pace and cohesion. The nadir came in a humiliating 3-0 defeat at home to Bournemouth in late January, followed by a limp 1-1 draw against relegation-threatened Southampton.

The Europa League has provided some respite, with United progressing to the knockout stages, but even there performances have been unconvincing. A fortunate 2-1 aggregate victory over a moderate Rangers side papered over significant cracks in both defensive organization and attacking fluency.

## Tactical Mismatch

Amorim's insistence on playing three at the back has exposed the squad's lack of suitable personnel. Centre-backs Lisandro Martinez and Matthijs de Ligt have looked uncomfortable in the wider roles of a back three, while the midfield partnership of Manuel Ugarte and Casemiro has lacked the energy and mobility to implement the high-pressing game the manager demands.

"The players are trying, but this system doesn't suit them," admitted former United captain Gary Neville on Sky Sports. "You look at this squad and it's been assembled for a back four, for counter-attacking football. Asking them to play Amorim's way is like trying to fit a square peg in a round hole."

## Ownership Under Scrutiny

The crisis has reignited debate about the Glazer family's ownership and Sir Jim Ratcliffe's INEOS management of football operations. The controversial decision to sack Ten Hag after significant summer investment, including the £85 million signing of Joshua Zirkzee, now looks increasingly ill-judged. The Dutchman has since taken charge of Bayern Munich and overseen a remarkable turnaround in their fortunes.

Ratcliffe's cost-cutting measures have also come under fire. The departure of experienced sporting director Dan Ashworth after just five months in the role left the club without clear strategic direction during a crucial transfer window. January arrivals were limited to the loan signing of young Portuguese midfielder Joao Neves, who has made only sporadic appearances.

## The Path Forward

With the club unlikely to qualify for European competition next season, speculation about Amorim's future has intensified. Bookmakers have slashed odds on former Real Madrid manager Carlo Ancelotti taking over, while England boss Thomas Tuchel has also been linked with a return to club management at Old Trafford.

For now, Amorim remains defiant. "I believe in my ideas," he insisted after the Southampton draw. "We need time to implement the changes necessary to compete at the level Manchester United demands. I am confident we will see improvement."

With crucial matches against Liverpool and Arsenal looming, the Portuguese manager will need to see results quickly if he is to avoid becoming another casualty of the Old Trafford managerial revolving door.
