# Lead
Global food prices are climbing as instability in key producing regions threatens already-stressed supply chains, with analysts warning that consumers will face further cost pressures in the coming months.

# The Numbers
- Global food price index: Rising 3.2% month-over-month
- Grain prices: Wheat up 8%, corn up 5%
- Fertilizer costs: Up 12%
- Shipping costs: Container rates Shanghai-Dubai up 100%

# What's Driving Costs
1. Geopolitical instability: Black Sea grain corridor disrupted
2. Energy costs: Fertilizer production tied to natural gas prices
3. Shipping insurance: Maritime war risk cover cancellations
4. Currency fluctuations: Dollar strength affecting import costs

# What's Being Hidden
- The true inflation numbers don't capture food quality degradation
- Retailers are shrinking package sizes (shrinkflation)
- Food banks are seeing unprecedented demand

# The Kicker
Every geopolitical flashpoint hits families twice: once in the headlines, again at the checkout line.
