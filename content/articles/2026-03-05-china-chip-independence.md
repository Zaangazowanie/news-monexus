# Lead

US export controls were supposed to strangle China's chip industry. Instead, they triggered a "whole-of-nation effort" that's producing breakthroughs — and raising questions about whether sanctions accelerate the very competition they're meant to prevent.

# What the Controls Did

- October 2022: Biden admin restricted advanced chip exports
- October 2023/December 2024: Controls tightened
- March 2025: Trump admin expanded restrictions
- Allies joined via coordinated framework

# What China Did Instead

- **Huawei:** Now using 33 China-sourced components (vs 5 foreign) in Pura 70 phones
- **Research output:** China producing 2x more chip research papers than US
- **DeepSeek R1 (Jan 2025):** Matched US AI models with fewer resources
- **Peking University (March 2025):** 2D transistor 40% faster than TSMC 3nm
- **Carbon nanotube chip:** First AI chip using ternary logic system

# The Paradox

Controls briefly disrupted China's chip ecosystem. But also "prompted China to implement an all-out, government-backed effort" for self-sufficiency. Chinese firms developing "workarounds" that may be less efficient but enable competitive products.

# Incentive Mapping

- **US:** Maintain tech lead via restrictions
- **China:** Achieve independence at any cost
- **Western chip firms:** Lost China revenue, less visibility into Chinese advances
- **Global supply chains:** Fragmenting into two ecosystems

# Critical Questions

1. Do export controls slow China down, or just redirect their innovation path?
2. What happens when China no longer needs Western chips?
3. Is the US fighting the last war (Cold War tech controls) with the wrong tools?

# Kicker

Export controls are a short-term fix creating long-term competitors. The question isn't whether China catches up — it's what happens when they do.
